Fun fact, a flock of flamingos is called a Flamboyance!

Did you know the word "flamingo" comes from the Spanish and Latin word "flamenco" which means fire, and it refers to the bright color of the birds' feathers.

 

Flamingos hold their bent bills upside down while they are feeding, often for several hours a day, so that they can filter out their food while skimming the water.

 

Fun fact, flamingo chicks are born gray or white and can take up to three years to reach their mature pink, orange, or red .
