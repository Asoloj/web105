Flamingos or flamingoes are a type of wading bird in the family of Phoenicopteridae, which is the only bird family in the order Phoenicopteriformes.

There are four flamingo species are distributed throughout the Americas, including the Caribbean, and also two species that are native to Africa, Asia, and Europe.

The colors for Flamingos range from various shades of pink all the way to a crimson red. Some of them are shades of orange too. They also have some colors of cream and white mixed in.

Flamingos prefer to be together in groups, that's why they are often seen in large numbers

Flamingos do more flying than most people probably think. They can take off quickly and reach speeds of up to 35 miles per hour.

The life span of a Flamingo is usually around 25 to 30 years.
